Sect. I · What it makes

Three output types.
One batch queue.

Drop videos or folders, pick what to produce, and walk away. Everything runs through your local ffmpeg — nothing leaves the machine.

01

Contact sheets

Grid of timestamped frames at configurable rows × columns. Ideal for archival indexes or preview catalogs. PNG or JPEG with optional header and per-cell time overlay.

PNG · JPEG · custom grid
02

Screenshots

Individual frames at evenly-spaced or custom timestamps. Full-resolution, numbered, named after the source. Useful for thumbnails, dailies, or reference captures.

PNG · JPEG · batch
03

Animated previews

Short clips stitched into a reel (WebP, WebM, or GIF) or laid out as an animated grid where every cell loops motion. Motion at a glance — without opening each file.

WebP · WebM · GIF · reel + grid
Sect. II · Why it exists

Built for real work.

Four decisions that shape how Mosaic behaves under load.

i.

Batch queue, not single files

Drop folders. Per-file progress, running live. Cancel at any point without corrupting in-flight outputs.

ii.

HDR → SDR, automatic

HDR10, HLG, and Dolby Vision videos produce clean SDR thumbnails without a manual tonemap step. Dolby Vision Profile 5 is color-corrected in-app.

iii.

Local-only, no telemetry

Your videos never leave the machine. Zero analytics. The only network call is an update check against GitHub's public API.

iv.

Auto-updates, signed

One-click install on new releases. Every update is cryptographically verified on-device against the app's embedded public key.

Sect. III · The app

Minimal by design.

Dark by default, light-mode aware. Follows your system preference. Uses the system font stack so it feels native on every platform.
